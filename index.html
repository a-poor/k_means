k-Means Clustering Visualization

by Austin Poor

k-Means: 20 n-Points: 10,000

Visualization of the k-Means Clustering Algorithm. Use the sliders above to adjust the value of k and the number of points. The algorithm partitions all n points into k clusters by:

  1. Picking k random points from the pool to use as the inital means
  2. Grouping the rest of the points by the mean they're closest to
  3. moving each mean to the center of their clusters
  4. repeating steps 2 and 3 until the assignments no longer change

When convergence has been reached – and the means haven't moved from one step to the next – the red box around the perimiter will turn green.
